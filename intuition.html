Appendix A.4

Intuition for the OSCAR rotation

OSCAR applies R_K = U_Q HHad P_K before INT2 quantization. The three factors are ordered to address two coupled quantities: the attention-importance metric R⊤C_QR and the token-wise INT2 residual covariance E_K.

||QK⊤ - QK̂⊤||F2 = tr(R⊤CQR · EK)

What each factor does

1

U_Q: expose attention importance

Eigendecomposing C_Q = Q⊤Q = U_QΛ_QU_Q⊤ makes the importance metric diagonal. Error in channel j is weighted by λ_j, so high-query-energy directions are visible to the quantizer design.

2

HHad: equalize and mix

After the U_Q basis, Hadamard conjugation gives diag(H⊤Λ_QH) = tr(C_Q)/d. The same mixing also spreads per-token outliers across channels, reducing the max-min range used by token-wise INT2 scaling.

3

P_K: balance quantization groups

Bit-reversal places large-eigenvalue directions into different contiguous groups. For power-of-two group sizes, each group receives a balanced sample of the importance hierarchy rather than a cluster of hard-to-quantize directions.

Why not rotate by K⊤K?

The key cache is consumed through QK⊤, not through raw Euclidean reconstruction of K. On Qwen3-8B, the eigenbases of Q⊤Q and K⊤K are nearly uncorrelated. A rotation that smooths key variance can therefore miss the directions where key error changes attention logits.

This is why OSCAR uses Q⊤Q for keys and V⊤S⊤SV for values.

Qwen3-4B-Thinking, layer 10, d = 128, key group size G_K = 64, 8000 calibration tokens. The table shows what the token-wise INT2 quantizer sees after each candidate rotation.

The point is not that one factor is universally sufficient. U_Q gives the attention metric, HHad equalizes that metric and spreads token outliers, and P_K improves the actual group layout used by the INT2 kernel.
